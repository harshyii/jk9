## <i class="bi bi-wrench-adjustable-circle"></i> What is an Impact Wrench?

<p>

An <strong>impact wrench</strong>, also called an impact gun, impact driver (heavy-duty type), or torque wrench machine, is a power tool specifically designed to tighten and loosen bolts and nuts using high rotational torque combined with rapid hammering action.

</p>

<p>

Unlike a standard drill, an impact wrench delivers bursts of rotational force, making it possible to remove rusted, seized, or over-tightened fasteners without excessive physical effort.

</p>

# <i class="bi bi-lightning-charge-fill text-warning"></i> Understanding Torque Ratings

<p>

Torque is the twisting force produced by an impact wrench. Higher torque means the tool can loosen tighter, rusted, or larger fasteners with less effort.

</p>

# Frequently Asked Questions

<h5>Can an impact wrench remove wheel nuts?</h5>

<p>

Yes. A quality 1/2-inch impact wrench with around 500–700 Nm torque can easily remove most passenger vehicle wheel nuts.

</p>

<h5>Do I need a compressor?</h5>

<p>

Only if you're buying a pneumatic impact wrench. Corded and cordless models work without an [air compressor](blog.html?id=air-compressor-buying-guide).

</p>

<h5>Is a brushless motor worth the extra cost?</h5>

<p>

For professionals and frequent users, absolutely. Brushless motors last longer, generate less heat, and offer improved efficiency.

</p>

<h5>Can I use regular sockets?</h5>

<p>

No. Always use impact sockets designed to handle repeated hammering forces.

</p>
