## What is a Pressure Washer?

<p>

A <strong>pressure washer</strong> is a powerful cleaning machine that sprays water at high pressure to remove dirt, mud, grease, algae, paint, oil, cement residue, and other contaminants from various surfaces.

Unlike a normal garden hose, a pressure washer combines high pressure with controlled water flow, making cleaning significantly faster while reducing water consumption.

</p>

<div id="psi"

<div class="accordion-body">

<ul>

<li><strong>90–120 Bar</strong> — Home cleaning</li>

<li><strong>130–170 Bar</strong> — Cars & bikes</li>

<li><strong>180–220 Bar</strong> — Workshops</li>

<li><strong>250+ Bar</strong> — Industrial cleaning</li>

</ul>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="accordion-item">

<h2 class="accordion-header">

<button
class="accordion-button collapsed"

</button>

</h2>

<div id="flow"
class="accordion-collapse collapse"
data-bs-parent="#specAccordion">

<div class="accordion-body">

Many buyers ignore water flow.

Higher pressure without enough water cannot clean efficiently.

A higher LPM allows dirt to be flushed away faster.

<ul>

<li>5–7 LPM → Home Use</li>

<li>8–10 LPM → Commercial</li>

<li>11–18 LPM → Heavy Duty</li>

</ul>

<h2 class="mt-5">

Pressure Washer Safety Tips

</h2>

<div class="alert alert-warning">

High-pressure water can cause serious injury if used incorrectly.

</div>

<div class="row g-3">

<div class="col-md-6">

<div class="card border-warning h-100">

<div class="card-body">

<ul>

<li>Always wear safety goggles.</li>

<li>Use waterproof footwear.</li>

<li>Never point the spray at people.</li>

<li>Disconnect power before servicing.</li>

<li>Keep children away.</li>

</ul>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="col-md-6">

<div class="card border-success h-100">

<div class="card-body">
