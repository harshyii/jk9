## Why Diamond Cutting Discs are Better

Diamond cutting discs don't actually cut with exposed diamonds like a knife.

Instead, tiny industrial diamonds embedded inside metal segments continuously grind away the material while new diamonds become exposed as the blade wears.

This provides:

<ul class="list-group list-group-flush mb-4">

<li class="list-group-item">
✅ Faster cutting speed
</li>

<li class="list-group-item">
✅ Better cutting accuracy
</li>

<li class="list-group-item">
✅ Less dust generation
</li>

<li class="list-group-item">
✅ Reduced operator fatigue
</li>

<li class="list-group-item">
✅ Longer blade life
</li>

<li class="list-group-item">
✅ Lower overall cutting cost
</li>

</ul>

## Wet Cutting vs Dry Cutting

One of the most common mistakes professionals make is using the wrong cutting method for their diamond blade.

<div class="row g-4">

<div class="col-md-6">

<div class="card border-primary shadow-sm h-100">

<div class="card-header bg-primary text-white">

💧 Wet Cutting

</div>

<div class="card-body">

<ul>

<li>Produces less dust</li>

<li>Keeps blade cool</li>

<li>Longest blade life</li>

<li>Smoother cutting finish</li>

<li>Recommended for granite, marble and concrete</li>

</ul>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="col-md-6">

<div class="card border-warning shadow-sm h-100">

<div class="card-header bg-warning">

🔥 Dry Cutting

</div>

<div class="card-body">

<ul>

<li>Ideal for portable jobs</li>

<li>No water source required</li>

<li>Short cutting intervals recommended</li>

<li>Allow blade to cool between cuts</li>

<li>Best with segmented blades</li>

</ul>

</div>

</div>

</div>

## Understanding Maximum RPM

Every diamond blade has a maximum safe operating speed.

Running above this speed can permanently damage the blade and create a serious safety hazard.

<div class="table-responsive">

| Blade Size | Typical Maximum RPM |
|------------|--------------------:|
| 4 inch (105 mm) | 14,500 RPM |
| 4.5 inch (115 mm) | 13,300 RPM |
| 5 inch (125 mm) | 12,200 RPM |
| 7 inch (180 mm) | 8,500 RPM |
| 9 inch (230 mm) | 6,600 RPM |
| 14 inch (355 mm) | 5,400 RPM |

</div>

<div class="alert alert-danger">

<strong>Safety Warning:</strong>

Always verify that your machine's RPM does not exceed the maximum RPM printed on the blade.

</div>

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

<div class="list-group mb-4">

<div class="list-group-item">

❌ Using a blade designed for tiles on reinforced concrete.

</div>

<div class="list-group-item">

❌ Applying excessive pressure while cutting.

</div>

<div class="list-group-item">

❌ Ignoring maximum RPM limits.

</div>

<div class="list-group-item">

❌ Continuing to use a damaged or cracked blade.

</div>

<div class="list-group-item">

❌ Installing the blade in the wrong rotation direction.

</div>

<div class="list-group-item">

❌ Dry cutting continuously without cooling.

</div>

</div>

## Maintenance Tips

<div class="card shadow-sm border-success mb-4">

<div class="card-body">

<ul>

<li>Clean the blade after every job.</li>

<li>Store in a dry place.</li>

<li>Inspect for cracks before every use.</li>

<li>Dress glazed blades using dressing stone.</li>

<li>Never drop or bend a diamond blade.</li>

<li>Replace worn-out blades before segments disappear.</li>

</ul>
