<h1>Drill Bit Selection Guide: Choose the Right Drill Bit for Every Material</h1>

<p>Using the correct drill bit is just as important as choosing the right drill. The wrong bit produces rough holes, wears out quickly and may even damage your machine. This guide explains different drill bit types, their applications and how to select the best one for every job.</p>

<hr>

<h2>Why Drill Bit Selection Matters</h2>

<p>Every material requires a different cutting geometry, hardness and coating. A drill bit designed for wood cannot efficiently drill hardened steel, while a masonry bit performs poorly on aluminium.</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning">
<strong>Using the wrong drill bit can lead to:</strong>
<ul class="mb-0 mt-2">
<li>Broken drill bits</li>
<li>Poor hole quality</li>
<li>Overheating</li>
<li>Reduced drilling speed</li>
<li>Motor overload</li>
<li>Unsafe operation</li>
</ul>
</div>

<h2>Choosing the Correct Size</h2>

<p>Select the drill bit diameter according to the screw, anchor or fastener you intend to install. Oversized holes reduce holding strength, while undersized holes make installation difficult.</p>

<div class="alert alert-info">
<strong>Tip:</strong> Always mark the drilling depth using masking tape or a drill stop to avoid drilling deeper than necessary.
</div>

<hr>

<h2>Tips for Longer Drill Bit Life</h2>

<div class="list-group">

<div class="list-group-item">✔ Use the correct drilling speed.</div>
<div class="list-group-item">✔ Apply steady pressure instead of excessive force.</div>
<div class="list-group-item">✔ Use cutting oil while drilling metal.</div>
<div class="list-group-item">✔ Keep drill bits clean after use.</div>
<div class="list-group-item">✔ Store bits in a dedicated drill bit case.</div>
<div class="list-group-item">✔ Replace dull bits before they overheat.</div>
