## What is a Grease Gun?

A grease gun is a lubrication tool designed to deliver grease under pressure into bearings, joints, bushings, gears, shafts, suspension components, and other moving mechanical parts.

Instead of manually applying grease with your fingers or a brush, a grease gun injects grease directly into grease nipples (zerk fittings), ensuring the lubricant reaches the internal moving surfaces.

<div class="alert alert-info">

<strong>Why it matters:</strong>

Proper lubrication reduces friction, protects components from corrosion, prevents overheating, and significantly increases machine life.

</div>

## Types of Grease Guns

### 1. Manual Grease Gun

<div class="card shadow-sm mb-4">

<div class="card-body">

Manual grease guns operate using hand pressure.

Every squeeze pushes grease through the hose into the lubrication point.

<strong>Advantages</strong>

<ul>

<li>Affordable</li>

<li>Portable</li>

<li>No electricity required</li>

<li>Easy to maintain</li>

<li>Ideal for workshops</li>

</ul>

<strong>Best For</strong>

<ul>

<li>Cars</li>

<li>Motorcycles</li>

<li>Farm equipment</li>

<li>DIY maintenance</li>

</ul>

</div>

</div>

---

### 2. Lever Grease Gun

The most popular industrial grease gun.

It uses a long lever that provides higher pressure with less effort.

<div class="alert alert-success">

Excellent for heavy machinery where grease fittings require high pressure.

</div>

---

### 3. Pistol Grip Grease Gun

Designed for one-handed operation.

Perfect when the other hand is needed to hold the hose or stabilize yourself while working.

Ideal for:

<ul>

<li>Vehicle maintenance</li>

<li>Agricultural equipment</li>

<li>Construction sites</li>

<li>Field repairs</li>

</ul>

---

### 4. Pneumatic Grease Gun

Powered by compressed air.

No manual pumping is required.

Advantages include:

<ul>

<li>Very fast lubrication</li>

<li>Consistent grease delivery</li>

<li>Reduced operator fatigue</li>

<li>Perfect for workshops</li>

<li>High productivity</li>

</ul>

---

### 5. Battery Powered Grease Gun

The premium option for professionals.

Cordless grease guns use rechargeable batteries to automatically pump grease under high pressure.

## How to Use a Grease Gun Properly

Using a grease gun correctly ensures every lubrication point receives the right amount of grease without damaging seals or wasting lubricant.

<div class="card shadow-sm border-success mb-4">

<div class="card-body">

<h4 class="text-success">
<i class="bi bi-list-check"></i> Step-by-Step Lubrication Process
</h4>

<ol>

<li>Clean the grease fitting using a clean cloth.</li>

<li>Select the correct grease recommended by the equipment manufacturer.</li>

<li>Load the grease cartridge properly.</li>

<li>Attach the grease gun coupler securely to the grease nipple.</li>

<li>Pump grease slowly and steadily.</li>

<li>Stop when fresh grease begins to appear or resistance increases.</li>

<li>Remove the coupler carefully.</li>

<li>Wipe away excess grease.</li>

<li>Store the grease gun in a clean, dry place.</li>

</ol>

</div>

## How to Load a Grease Cartridge

<div class="row g-4">

<div class="col-lg-6">

<div class="card h-100 shadow-sm">

<div class="card-body">

<h5>
Method 1 — Cartridge Loading
</h5>

<ul>

<li>Unscrew the grease gun barrel.</li>

<li>Remove the empty cartridge.</li>

<li>Insert the new cartridge.</li>

<li>Remove the plastic seal.</li>

<li>Reassemble the barrel.</li>

<li>Release the plunger rod.</li>

<li>Prime the grease gun.</li>

</ul>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="col-lg-6">

<div class="card h-100 shadow-sm">

<div class="card-body">

<h5>
Method 2 — Bulk Filling
</h5>

<ul>

<li>Pull back the plunger.</li>

<li>Fill grease directly from a grease bucket.</li>

<li>Avoid trapping air.</li>

<li>Seal the barrel tightly.</li>

<li>Prime before use.</li>

</ul>

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

<div class="alert alert-danger">

<h5>
<i class="bi bi-exclamation-triangle-fill"></i>
Avoid These Mistakes
</h5>

<ul class="mb-0">

<li>Using the wrong grease type.</li>

<li>Over-greasing bearings.</li>

<li>Mixing incompatible greases.</li>

<li>Skipping lubrication schedules.</li>

<li>Using damaged grease hoses.</li>

<li>Applying grease too quickly.</li>

<li>Leaving grease exposed to dust.</li>

## Safety Tips

<div class="alert alert-warning">

<ul class="mb-0">

<li>Wear safety gloves.</li>

<li>Use safety glasses during lubrication.</li>

<li>Never exceed recommended grease pressure.</li>

<li>Keep grease away from open flames.</li>

<li>Dispose of used cartridges responsibly.</li>

<li>Read the equipment manufacturer's lubrication schedule.</li>

<li>Do not lubricate moving machinery unless specifically designed for it.</li>

</ul>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<div class="accordion-item">

<h2 class="accordion-header">

<button class="accordion-button" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#faq1">

How often should I grease machinery?

</button>

</h2>

<div id="faq1" class="accordion-collapse collapse show">

Lubrication intervals depend on equipment type, operating conditions, and manufacturer recommendations. Heavy-duty machinery may require daily lubrication, while light-duty equipment may only need servicing every few months.

</div>

<div class="accordion-item">

<h2 class="accordion-header">

<button class="accordion-button collapsed" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#faq2">

Can different grease types be mixed?

</button>

</h2>

<div id="faq2" class="accordion-collapse collapse">

<div class="accordion-body">

Generally, no. Mixing incompatible grease formulations can reduce lubrication performance and damage bearings.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="accordion-item">

Why won't my grease gun pump?

Common causes include trapped air, blocked grease fittings, clogged hoses, empty cartridges, or damaged couplers.
