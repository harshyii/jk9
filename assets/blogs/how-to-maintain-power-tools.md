<h2 class="fw-bold mb-3">
Why Regular Maintenance Matters
</h2>

<p>
Every power tool operates under heavy mechanical load. Dust, heat, vibration and moisture slowly reduce performance over time. Without routine maintenance, motors work harder, bearings wear faster and electrical components become unreliable.
</p>

<p>
A few minutes of maintenance after every job can significantly increase the lifespan of your equipment while reducing downtime and repair expenses.
</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning border-start border-5 mt-3">

<strong>Professional Tip</strong>

<p class="mb-0 mt-2">
Most power tool failures occur because of poor maintenance rather than manufacturing defects.
</p>

</div>

<h2 class="fw-bold mb-3">
Daily Maintenance Checklist
</h2>

<div class="list-group mb-3">

<div class="list-group-item">
✔ Remove dust and debris from the tool body.
</div>

<div class="list-group-item">
✔ Clean ventilation openings.
</div>

<div class="list-group-item">
✔ Inspect the power cable or battery.
</div>

<div class="list-group-item">
✔ Check switches for smooth operation.
</div>

<div class="list-group-item">
✔ Wipe the tool using a dry microfiber cloth.
</div>

<div class="list-group-item">
✔ Store the tool in a dry, dust-free location.
</div>

</div>

Common Maintenance Mistakes
</h2>

<div class="row g-4">

<div class="col-md-6">

<div class="card border-danger h-100">

<div class="card-body">

<h5 class="text-danger">
Avoid These
</h5>

<ul class="mb-0">

<li>Ignoring unusual vibration</li>

<li>Blocking ventilation holes</li>

<li>Using damaged cutting discs</li>

<li>Operating with worn brushes</li>

<li>Using the wrong accessories</li>

<li>Storing tools while dirty</li>

</ul>

</div>

</div>

<div class="col-md-6">

<div class="card border-success h-100">

Best Practices
</h5>

<ul class="mb-0">

<li>Clean after every use</li>

<li>Store in protective cases</li>

<li>Replace worn accessories early</li>

<li>Use genuine spare parts</li>
