<h2>
What is an Impact Drill?
</h2>

<p>

An <strong>Impact Drill</strong>, also known as a Hammer Drill, combines normal rotary drilling with a mechanical hammering action. Two internal gear plates rapidly strike each other, creating small impacts while the drill rotates.

This makes it suitable for drilling into:

</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning py-2">

<ul class="mb-0">

<li>Brick Walls</li>

<li>Wood</li>

<li>Steel</li>

<li>Aluminium</li>

<li>Plastic</li>

<li>Light Concrete</li>

<li>Furniture Installation</li>

</ul>

</div>

<p>

Because it accepts standard drill bits, an impact drill is considered one of the most versatile tools for homeowners and maintenance professionals.

</p>

<hr>

<h2>
What is a Rotary Hammer?
</h2>

<p>

A <strong>Rotary Hammer</strong> is a heavy-duty drilling machine that uses a pneumatic piston system instead of simple mechanical gears. The piston produces powerful impacts with minimal effort from the operator.

It is designed specifically for:

</p>

<div class="alert alert-success py-2">

<ul class="mb-0">

<li>Concrete Drilling</li>

<li>Stone</li>

<li>Granite</li>

<li>Masonry</li>

<li>Anchor Installation</li>

<li>Industrial Construction</li>

<li>Tile Removal</li>

<li>Concrete Chiselling</li>

</ul>

</div>

<p>

Most rotary hammers use <strong>SDS Plus</strong> or <strong>SDS Max</strong> drill bits that transfer impact energy far more efficiently than standard drill chucks.

</p>

<h2>
How Do They Work?
</h2>

<div class="row g-4">

<div class="col-md-6">

<div class="card h-100 shadow-sm">

<div class="card-body">

<h4>
Impact Drill
</h4>

<p>

The hammer action is created by two toothed discs sliding over each other while rotating.

</p>

<p>

This provides thousands of small impacts per minute but requires the user to apply pressure while drilling.

</p>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="col-md-6">

<div class="card h-100 shadow-sm">

<div class="card-body">

<h4>
Rotary Hammer
</h4>

<p>

A compressed air piston strikes the drill mechanism internally, producing much stronger impact energy.

</p>

<p>

The machine performs most of the work, allowing faster drilling with significantly less effort.

</p>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<h2>
Limitations
</h2>

<div class="row g-4">

<div class="col-md-6">

<div class="alert alert-warning">

<h5>
Impact Drill
</h5>

<ul class="mb-0">

<li>Slower in reinforced concrete</li>

<li>Requires more physical pressure</li>

<li>Not suitable for demolition work</li>

<li>Produces more vibration while drilling hard surfaces</li>

</ul>

</div>

</div>

<div class="alert alert-danger">

<h5>
Rotary Hammer
</h5>
