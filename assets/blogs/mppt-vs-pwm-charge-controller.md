<h1>MPPT vs PWM Charge Controller: Which One Should You Buy?</h1>

<p>A solar charge controller protects your battery while regulating power from solar panels. The two most common technologies are <strong>MPPT (Maximum Power Point Tracking)</strong> and <strong>PWM (Pulse Width Modulation)</strong>. Although both perform the same basic task, their efficiency, performance and cost differ significantly. This guide explains which controller is best for your solar installation.</p>

<h2>What is a PWM Charge Controller?</h2>

<p>PWM controllers directly connect the solar panel to the battery using rapid switching. They reduce charging current as the battery approaches full charge, preventing overcharging.</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning py-2">
<strong>Best Applications</strong>
<ul class="mb-0 mt-2">
<li>Small home solar systems</li>
<li>Street lights</li>
<li>Garden lighting</li>
<li>Battery backup systems</li>
<li>Budget solar installations</li>
</ul>
</div>

<p>PWM controllers work best when the solar panel voltage closely matches the battery voltage.</p>

<hr>

<h2>What is an MPPT Charge Controller?</h2>

<p>MPPT technology continuously tracks the maximum power point of a solar panel and converts excess voltage into additional charging current. This allows the battery to receive maximum available energy throughout the day.</p>

<div class="alert alert-success py-2">
<strong>Ideal Applications</strong>
<ul class="mb-0 mt-2">
<li>Residential rooftop solar</li>
<li>Commercial solar plants</li>
<li>Industrial backup systems</li>
<li>Off-grid solar installations</li>
<li>High-capacity battery banks</li>
</ul>
</div>
