<h2 class="mt-5">
What is an On-Grid Solar System?
</h2>

<p>

An <strong>On-Grid Solar System</strong>, also known as a
<strong>Grid-Tied Solar System</strong>, is directly connected to your local
electricity distribution network.

During the day, electricity generated from solar panels powers your home or
business. Any excess electricity is exported to the grid through
<strong>Net Metering</strong>, allowing you to earn credits that reduce your
future electricity bills.

At night or during cloudy weather, electricity is automatically supplied from
the grid.

</p>

<h2 class="mt-5">

What is an Off-Grid Solar System?

</h2>

<p>

An <strong>Off-Grid Solar System</strong> operates completely independent of
the electricity grid.

Instead of exporting power, excess electricity is stored inside
<strong>solar batteries</strong> for use during nighttime or bad weather.

Because of battery storage, off-grid systems continue supplying electricity
even when the grid is unavailable.

</p>

<h4>

Buying Tip

</h4>

<p class="mb-0">

If your property already has a reliable electricity connection,
an <strong>On-Grid Solar System</strong> usually offers the fastest return on
investment because it eliminates most of your electricity bill through
Net Metering.

If electricity is unavailable or highly unreliable, an
<strong>Off-Grid Solar System</strong> provides complete energy independence.

<h2>
Cost Comparison: Off-Grid vs On-Grid Solar
</h2>

<p>

One of the biggest differences between these two systems is the overall installation cost. While solar panels are similar, off-grid systems require batteries, charge controllers, and additional electrical protection, making them significantly more expensive.

<h2>

Frequently Asked Questions

</h2>

<div class="accordion" id="faq">

<div class="accordion-item">

<h2 class="accordion-header">

<button class="accordion-button" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#faq1">

Can an on-grid solar system work during a power cut?

</button>

</h2>

<div id="faq1" class="accordion-collapse collapse show">

<div class="accordion-body">

No. Standard on-grid systems automatically shut down during a power outage to protect utility workers. Backup requires batteries or a hybrid inverter.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="accordion-item">

<h2 class="accordion-header">

<button class="accordion-button collapsed" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#faq2">

Which system saves more money?

</button>

</h2>

<div id="faq2" class="accordion-collapse collapse">

<div class="accordion-body">

On-grid solar generally provides better financial returns because there is no battery replacement cost and Net Metering offsets electricity bills.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="accordion-item">

<h2 class="accordion-header">

<button class="accordion-button collapsed" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#faq3">

Can I upgrade from off-grid to on-grid later?

</button>

</h2>

<div id="faq3" class="accordion-collapse collapse">

<div class="accordion-body">

Yes, but additional equipment such as a grid-tie inverter and Net Metering approval may be required.
