<h1>Pressure Washer Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right Pressure Washer</h1>

<p>A pressure washer makes cleaning vehicles, driveways, machinery, buildings and industrial equipment significantly faster than manual cleaning. Choosing the correct machine depends on pressure, flow rate, motor type and intended application. This guide explains everything you should know before purchasing a pressure washer.</p>

<hr>

<h2>What is a Pressure Washer?</h2>

<p>A pressure washer uses a high-pressure pump to force water through a narrow nozzle. The concentrated water stream removes mud, grease, dust, paint, algae and stubborn dirt from almost any hard surface.</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning py-2">
<strong>Common Applications</strong>
<ul class="mb-0 mt-2">
<li>Car & Bike Washing</li>
<li>Industrial Equipment Cleaning</li>
<li>Building Exterior Cleaning</li>
<li>Driveways & Parking Areas</li>
<li>Agricultural Machinery</li>
<li>Solar Panel Cleaning</li>
</ul>
</div>

<h2>Understanding PSI</h2>

<p>PSI (Pounds per Square Inch) represents the cleaning pressure. Higher PSI removes stubborn dirt faster but isn't suitable for every surface.</p>

<h2>Common Buying Mistakes</h2>

<div class="alert alert-danger">

<ul class="mb-0">

<li>Buying only based on PSI.</li>
<li>Ignoring water flow (LPM).</li>
<li>Using incorrect nozzle angles.</li>
<li>Purchasing poor-quality pumps.</li>
<li>Choosing machines without service support.</li>
<li>Using extension cables with high-power models.</li>

</ul>

</div>

<hr>

<h2>Maintenance Tips</h2>

<div class="list-group">

<div class="list-group-item">✔ Always flush clean water after detergent use.</div>

<div class="list-group-item">✔ Never run the machine without water supply.</div>

<div class="list-group-item">✔ Inspect hoses and fittings regularly.</div>

<div class="list-group-item">✔ Clean inlet filters frequently.</div>

<div class="list-group-item">✔ Store indoors to avoid freezing or moisture damage.</div>

<div class="list-group-item">✔ Replace worn nozzles and seals on time.</div>

</div>

Yes, but only at low pressure using a wide-angle nozzle and from a safe distance to prevent panel damage.

</button>

<div id="faq3" class="accordion-collapse collapse">

Both matter. PSI provides cleaning force, while LPM determines how quickly dirt is washed away. A balanced combination delivers the best performance.
