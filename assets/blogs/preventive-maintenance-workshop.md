<h1>Preventive Maintenance Checklist for Workshops and Industrial Equipment</h1>

<p>Unexpected equipment failure can stop production, increase repair costs and reduce workplace safety. A well-planned preventive maintenance program helps workshops maximize machine life, improve productivity and minimize downtime. This guide explains how to build an effective maintenance schedule for power tools and industrial equipment.</p>

<hr>

<h2>Why Preventive Maintenance Matters</h2>

<p>Preventive maintenance involves inspecting, cleaning and servicing equipment before problems occur. Rather than waiting for a breakdown, regular maintenance keeps machines operating efficiently throughout their service life.</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning py-2">
<strong>Key Benefits</strong>
<ul class="mb-0 mt-2">
<li>Reduced machine downtime</li>
<li>Lower repair costs</li>
<li>Longer equipment lifespan</li>
<li>Improved workplace safety</li>
<li>Higher productivity</li>
<li>Better energy efficiency</li>
</ul>
</div>

<h2>Signs Your Machine Needs Service</h2>

<div class="list-group mb-3">
<div class="list-group-item">⚠ Excessive vibration</div>
<div class="list-group-item">⚠ Burning smell</div>
<div class="list-group-item">⚠ Reduced cutting performance</div>
<div class="list-group-item">⚠ Unusual motor noise</div>
<div class="list-group-item">⚠ Overheating</div>
<div class="list-group-item">⚠ Frequent tripping</div>
<div class="list-group-item">⚠ Visible cable damage</div>
</div>

<p>Never continue operating equipment showing any of these symptoms. Early servicing prevents costly failures.</p>

<h2>Workshop Safety Tips</h2>

<div class="alert alert-success">
<ul class="mb-0">
<li>Disconnect power before servicing.</li>
<li>Wear gloves and safety glasses.</li>
<li>Use genuine replacement parts.</li>
<li>Keep maintenance records.</li>
<li>Store tools in dry, clean areas.</li>
<li>Train operators regularly.</li>
<li>Never bypass machine safety guards.</li>
</ul>
