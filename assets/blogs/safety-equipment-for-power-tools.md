<h1>Essential Safety Equipment for Using Power Tools</h1>

<p>Power tools make construction, fabrication and repair work faster and more efficient, but they can also cause serious injuries if proper safety equipment isn't used. Whether you're a DIY enthusiast, contractor or industrial professional, wearing the right Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) should always be your first priority.</p>

<hr>

<h2>Why PPE Matters</h2>

<p>Flying debris, sparks, dust, loud noise and accidental contact with moving parts are common hazards while using power tools. The right safety gear greatly reduces the risk of injuries and helps you work confidently.</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning">
<strong>Never operate a power tool without appropriate safety equipment.</strong>
</div>

<div class="col-md-6"><div class="card h-100"><div class="card-body">

<div class="col-md-6"><div class="card h-100"><div class="card-body">
<h5>❌ Damaged Power Cords</h5>

<div class="col-md-6"><div class="card h-100"><div class="card-body">

<h2>Emergency Response</h2>

<div class="table-responsive">

<table class="table table-striped">

<thead>

<tr>
<th>Incident</th>
<th>Immediate Action</th>
</tr>

</thead>

<tbody>

<tr>
<td>Eye Injury</td>
<td>Flush with clean water and seek medical attention immediately.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td>Electric Shock</td>
<td>Disconnect power before touching the victim and call emergency services.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td>Cut Injury</td>
<td>Apply pressure using a clean dressing and seek medical help if severe.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td>Dust Exposure</td>
<td>Move to fresh air and seek medical advice if breathing becomes difficult.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td>Burn Injury</td>
<td>Cool with clean running water and avoid applying oils or creams.</td>
</tr>

</tbody>

</table>

<h2>Best Practices for Safe Working</h2>

<div class="alert alert-primary">

<ul class="mb-0">

<li>Maintain complete focus while operating power tools.</li>
<li>Never bypass safety switches.</li>
<li>Disconnect power before changing accessories.</li>
<li>Keep children and bystanders away from the work area.</li>
<li>Store tools in a dry, secure location.</li>
<li>Follow the manufacturer's maintenance schedule.</li>
<li>Replace worn accessories immediately.</li>

</ul>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<div class="accordion" id="faq">

<div class="accordion-item">
<h2 class="accordion-header">
<button class="accordion-button" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#faq1">
Are safety glasses enough while using power tools?
</button>
</h2>
<div id="faq1" class="accordion-collapse collapse show">
<div class="accordion-body">
No. Depending on the tool, you may also require hearing protection, gloves, respirators and safety footwear.
</div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-item">
<h2 class="accordion-header">
<button class="accordion-button collapsed" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#faq2">
Should I wear gloves with every power tool?
</button>
</h2>
<div id="faq2" class="accordion-collapse collapse">
<div class="accordion-body">
Not always. Gloves improve protection for many tools but should not be worn where they may become entangled in rotating machinery.
</div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-item">
<h2 class="accordion-header">
<button class="accordion-button collapsed" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#faq3">
When should safety equipment be replaced?
</button>
</h2>
<div id="faq3" class="accordion-collapse collapse">
<div class="accordion-body">
Replace any PPE that is damaged, cracked, excessively worn or no longer provides proper protection.
</div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="accordion-item">
<h2 class="accordion-header">
<button class="accordion-button collapsed" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#faq4">
Can ordinary sunglasses replace safety glasses?
</button>
</h2>
<div id="faq4" class="accordion-collapse collapse">
<div class="accordion-body">
No. Safety glasses are impact-rated and specifically designed to protect against flying debris, unlike regular sunglasses.
</div>
</div>
</div>
