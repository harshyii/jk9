<p>

Grid electricity is supplied by your local electricity distribution company through overhead or underground transmission lines. Every appliance in your home, including televisions, fans, refrigerators and Wi-Fi routers, depends on this power supply unless an alternative energy source is available.

<h2>What Is Rooftop Solar?</h2>

<p>

A rooftop solar system converts sunlight into electricity using photovoltaic (PV) panels installed on your roof. During daylight hours, this electricity powers your home while reducing dependence on the electrical grid.

</p>

<p>

Depending on the type of system installed, excess electricity may be exported to the grid through net metering or stored in batteries for later use.

</p>

<div class="alert alert-info">

<strong>Did You Know?</strong>

<p class="mb-0 mt-2">

Modern solar panels usually have an expected service life of more than
<strong>25 years</strong>, making them one of the best long-term investments for reducing electricity costs.

</p>

<h2>How Does Net Metering Work?</h2>

<p>

One of the biggest advantages of an <strong>on-grid solar system</strong> is <strong>net metering</strong>. It allows excess electricity generated by your solar panels to be exported to the electricity grid. When your home or business requires additional electricity, power is automatically imported from the grid.

</p>

<div class="col-md-6">

<div class="card h-100">

<div class="card-body">

</p>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="col-md-6">

<div class="card h-100">

<div class="card-body">

<h5>🌙 During the Night</h5>

<p class="mb-0">

Electricity is automatically supplied from the utility grid whenever solar generation is unavailable.

</p>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="alert alert-info mt-3">

<strong>Did You Know?</strong>

<p class="mb-0 mt-2">

Net metering helps reduce your overall electricity bill by adjusting the energy you export against the energy you consume from the grid, subject to applicable utility regulations.

</p>
