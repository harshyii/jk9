<h2>1. Welding Helmet</h2>

<p>
A welding helmet is the most important safety accessory. It protects your eyes and face from intense arc light, sparks and flying metal particles.
</p>

<div class="row g-3">

<div class="col-md-6">

<div class="card h-100">

<li>Requires lifting between welds</li>

</ul>

<h2>3. Electrode Holder</h2>

<p>
The electrode holder securely grips welding electrodes while safely transferring current from the welding machine.
</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning">

Choose a holder with:

<ul class="mt-2 mb-0">

<li>Heavy-duty copper jaws</li>

<li>Heat-resistant insulation</li>

<li>Comfortable grip</li>

<li>Current rating matching your welding machine</li>

</ul>

</div>

<hr>

<h2>4. Earth Clamp</h2>

<p>
An earth clamp completes the electrical circuit by connecting the welding machine to the workpiece.
</p>

<p>
Poor quality clamps create unstable arcs, excessive heat and inconsistent weld quality.
</p>

<div class="card border-success">

<div class="card-body">

<strong>Buying Tip</strong>

<p class="mb-0">

Always choose solid brass or copper clamps with strong spring pressure for reliable electrical contact.

</p>

</div>

</div>

<hr>

<p>
Welding cables carry high electrical current from the machine to the electrode holder and earth clamp. Using undersized cables causes voltage drops and overheating.

<h2>6. Chipping Hammer</h2>

<p>
After shielded metal arc welding (SMAW), slag forms over the weld bead. A chipping hammer is used to remove this layer before inspection or additional welding.
</p>

<div class="row g-3">

<div class="col-md-6">
<div class="card h-100">
<div class="card-body">
<h5>Pointed End</h5>
<p class="mb-0">Removes stubborn slag from corners and deep joints.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="col-md-6">
<div class="card h-100">
<div class="card-body">
<h5>Flat End</h5>
<p class="mb-0">Quickly cleans larger flat welding surfaces.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>

</div>

<hr>

<h2>7. Wire Brush</h2>

<p>
A welding wire brush removes rust, slag and oxidation before and after welding. Cleaning the joint improves weld penetration and produces a stronger bond.
</p>

<div class="alert alert-info">
Use stainless steel wire brushes only for stainless steel work to avoid contamination.
</div>
