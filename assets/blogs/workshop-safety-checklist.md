<h1>Workshop Safety Checklist: 20 Essential Rules Every Technician Should Follow</h1>

<p>Whether you're a professional fabricator, electrician, mechanic or DIY enthusiast, workshop safety should always come before productivity. Most workplace injuries happen because of poor housekeeping, improper tool usage or failure to wear protective equipment. This checklist helps you build safer working habits and reduce the risk of accidents.</p>

<hr>

<h2>Why Workshop Safety Matters</h2>

<p>A clean and organized workshop protects workers, improves efficiency, extends tool life and helps avoid expensive damage. Developing safe working habits also reduces downtime caused by injuries.</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning"><strong>Remember:</strong> Safety equipment costs far less than medical treatment or replacing damaged machinery.</div>

<h2>Safe Power Tool Practices</h2>

<div class="list-group">

<div class="list-group-item">✔ Read the user manual before operating new equipment.</div>
<div class="list-group-item">✔ Never remove factory-installed safety guards.</div>
<div class="list-group-item">✔ Use the correct accessory for each machine.</div>
<div class="list-group-item">✔ Allow tools to reach full speed before cutting.</div>
<div class="list-group-item">✔ Never force a machine beyond its capacity.</div>
<div class="list-group-item">✔ Disconnect power before changing blades or bits.</div>

</div>

<hr>

<h2>Electrical Safety</h2>

<div class="alert alert-info">

<ul class="mb-0">
<li>Use properly grounded electrical outlets.</li>
<li>Do not overload extension boards.</li>
<li>Replace damaged plugs immediately.</li>
<li>Keep electrical equipment away from water.</li>
<li>Inspect cables regularly.</li>
<li>Switch off machines before unplugging.</li>
</ul>

</div>

<h2>Emergency Preparedness</h2>

<p>Every workshop should have clearly marked emergency exits, first aid kits, emergency contact numbers and accessible fire extinguishers. Workers should know evacuation procedures and basic first aid for burns, cuts and electrical shocks.</p>
