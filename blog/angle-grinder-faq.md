<p>Whether you're buying your first angle grinder or upgrading your existing tool, these frequently asked questions will help you choose, use, and maintain your angle grinder safely.</p>

---

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>What is an angle grinder used for?</summary>

An angle grinder is commonly used for:

- Cutting metal
- Grinding welds
- Removing rust
- Surface preparation
- Sharpening tools
- Tile and stone cutting
- Concrete cutting
- Polishing with suitable accessories

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>Which angle grinder size is best?</summary>

- **100 mm (4")** — Home users and DIY projects
- **115 mm (4.5")** — General workshop use
- **125 mm (5")** — Professional fabrication work
- **180 mm (7")** — Heavy-duty industrial work
- **230 mm (9")** — Concrete and structural steel cutting

Choose the size according to the type of work rather than simply selecting the largest grinder.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>What motor power should I choose?</summary>

- **650–850 W** — Household work
- **900–1200 W** — Workshop and fabrication
- **1400 W+** — Continuous industrial applications

Higher wattage is recommended only for demanding jobs.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>What is the difference between a grinding disc and a cutting disc?</summary>

A **grinding disc** is thicker and designed for removing material.

A **cutting disc** is thinner and should only be used for cutting.

Never use a cutting disc for grinding.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>Can an angle grinder cut stainless steel?</summary>

Yes.

Use an **INOX-rated cutting disc** designed specifically for stainless steel.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>Can I cut ceramic tiles or marble?</summary>

Yes.

Use a **diamond blade** suitable for ceramic, porcelain, marble, or granite.

For cleaner cuts:

- Mark the cutting line.
- Make shallow passes.
- Avoid forcing the blade.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>Can an angle grinder cut wood?</summary>

Standard abrasive discs should **never** be used on wood.

If wood cutting is required, use only blades specifically designed and approved for angle grinders.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>Is a cordless angle grinder better than a corded model?</summary>

**Cordless**

- Portable
- Ideal for outdoor work
- Suitable where power is unavailable

**Corded**

- Continuous operation
- Higher sustained power
- Better for heavy-duty applications

Choose based on where and how often you'll use the tool.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>What safety equipment should I wear?</summary>

Always wear:

- Safety goggles or face shield
- Hearing protection
- Dust mask when required
- Cut-resistant gloves
- Safety shoes

Avoid loose clothing and jewellery.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>Why is my angle grinder vibrating?</summary>

Common causes include:

- Damaged disc
- Incorrect installation
- Bent spindle
- Worn bearings
- Poor-quality accessories

Stop using the grinder immediately if excessive vibration occurs.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>When should grinding or cutting discs be replaced?</summary>

Replace a disc immediately if it is:

- Cracked
- Chipped
- Warped
- Excessively worn
- Damaged after being dropped
- Past its expiry date

Never use damaged abrasive discs.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>Can an angle grinder remove rust?</summary>

Yes.

Popular rust-removal accessories include:

- Wire cup brushes
- Wire wheels
- Flap discs
- Grinding wheels

Choose the accessory based on the amount of rust and required finish.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>How do I maintain an angle grinder?</summary>

Regular maintenance includes:

- Cleaning air vents
- Inspecting the power cord
- Tightening the guard
- Checking carbon brushes
- Cleaning the spindle
- Storing the tool in a dry place

Proper maintenance improves performance and extends tool life.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>How long does an angle grinder last?</summary>

A quality angle grinder can provide years of reliable service when:

- Used correctly
- Maintained regularly
- Fitted with quality accessories

Actual lifespan depends on workload and operating conditions.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>Can beginners use an angle grinder safely?</summary>

Yes.

Beginners should:

- Read the instruction manual
- Wear appropriate PPE
- Use the correct disc
- Hold the tool firmly with both hands
- Practice on scrap material before actual work

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>What accessories are commonly used with angle grinders?</summary>

Common accessories include:

- Cutting discs
- Grinding wheels
- Flap discs
- Diamond blades
- Wire cup brushes
- Wire wheels
- Sanding pads
- Polishing pads

Always ensure accessories are compatible with the grinder's disc size and speed rating.

</details>

<details class="faq-card">
<summary>Which angle grinder brand should I choose?</summary>

Compare brands based on:

- Build quality
- Motor power
- Safety features
- Warranty
- Spare parts availability
- Service network
- Availability of accessories

Choose a model that matches your intended applications rather than only the price.

</details>
