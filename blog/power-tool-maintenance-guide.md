<h2>Keep Tools Clean</h2>

<p>Dust, metal particles and debris reduce cooling efficiency and increase wear on motors and bearings.</p>

<div class="alert alert-success">
<strong>Recommended Cleaning Tools</strong>
<ul class="mt-2 mb-0">
<li>Soft paint brush</li>
<li>Compressed air</li>
<li>Microfiber cloth</li>
<li>Plastic-safe cleaner</li>
<li>Dry cotton cloth</li>
</ul>
</div>

<p>Avoid washing electric power tools with water or using excessive solvents near switches and motors.</p>

<hr>

<h2>Inspect the Power Cord</h2>

<p>A damaged power cable is one of the most common safety hazards.</p>

<div class="col-md-6">

<h2>Keep Air Vents Open</h2>

<p>Blocked ventilation causes overheating and reduces motor life.</p>

<div class="alert alert-info">
Always blow compressed air through cooling vents after heavy use. Never insert metal objects into ventilation openings.
</div>

<hr>

<h2>Replace Carbon Brushes</h2>

<p>Carbon brushes transfer electricity to the motor. As they wear down, the tool loses power and may produce excessive sparks.</p>

<div class="alert alert-warning">
Replace carbon brushes with genuine manufacturer-approved parts before they wear completely to avoid damaging the motor.
</div>

<div class="col-md-6">
<div class="card h-100">
<div class="card-body">

<ul class="mb-0">
<li>Loss of power</li>
<li>Excessive sparking</li>
<li>Motor stops intermittently</li>
<li>Burning smell</li>
<li>Difficulty starting</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="col-md-6">
<div class="card h-100">
<div class="card-body">
<h5>Inspection Interval</h5>
<p class="mb-0">Inspect carbon brushes every 100–150 operating hours or according to the manufacturer's maintenance schedule.</p>
</div>
</div>

<h2>Common Maintenance Mistakes</h2>

<div class="alert alert-danger">

Avoid these common mistakes:

<ul class="mt-2 mb-0">
<li>Running dull blades or drill bits</li>
<li>Blocking cooling vents</li>
<li>Using damaged extension cords</li>
<li>Ignoring unusual vibration</li>
<li>Using incorrect accessories</li>
<li>Skipping regular cleaning</li>
<li>Operating with loose fasteners</li>
<li>Ignoring unusual noises</li>
</ul>
